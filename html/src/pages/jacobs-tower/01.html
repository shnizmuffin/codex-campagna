{{#MarkdownIt}} Level One: Classic --- Level 1 is formed mostly out of large blocks of tan stone. Except for Rooms 2 and 3, the area is dry and cool. Sconces with flaming torches provide excellent lighting throughout the entire Level, excepting Rooms 9 and 10. All rooms are separated with simple, unlocked wooden doors unless otherwise noted, and all doors open away from Room 1. ### Room 1: Entrance > You find yourself in a cool, dry room made primarially of large blocks of tan stone. Sconces with flaming torches provide excellent lighting. Seven majestic red drapes with gold fringes line the north wall, covering the tan stone masonry. Thick wooden doors lie to the east and west. The entrance to Jacob’s Tower is empty of any hazards or traps, but it is not bare. The room is made of tan stone, but a great deal of animal feces, handfuls of tiny bones, and rotting bundles of hay all but completely cover the floor. Along the south wall is a small pool of putrid water. > > Dozens of diminutive animals - hedgehogs, mice, and rabbits - scatter away from you, into the hay bales. Three small animals - a dog, a pig, and a skunk - stand their ground; growling, snarling, and hissing at you, respectively. They look malnourished. Room 2 stinks. Once the door is open, all those within 15 feet must make a **Fortitude save (DC 11)** or be sickened for one round. The rotting bundles of hay count as difficult terrain. The animals inside, with blotchy fur and muddy faces, look like they are being starved and will immediately attack the adventurers. In the room are one skunk, one pig, and one dog which will immediately attack the adventurers. There are also dozens of smaller animals such as hedgehogs, mice and rabbits, but these will not attack, instead fleeing into the hay when the PCs arrive. Once the skunk, pig, and dog are killed, the smaller animals will come to feast on their corpses. These animals are not totally feral, and it is possible to woo them to the characters' side. A **Handle Animal check (DC 10)** as a standard action on any animal will stun him for a round. A **Handle Animal check (DC 15)** on any animal will cause him to slink away and abandon the fight. A **Handle Animal check (DC 20)** on any animal will cause him to join the characters for the remainder of the Level. With a **Perception check (DC 10)**, our heroes can notice that the pool of water actually goes below the wall to the south. One brave soul can take a dip in this putrid water and wriggle his way into Room 3. If the hero fails a **Swim check (DC 10)** while in the water, he must make a **Fortitude save (DC 13)** or contract [Cholera](http://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/afflictions/diseases/cholera) from the filth. {{/MarkdownIt}}

A wooden door at the end of the hallway is slightly ajar, but not open enough to see clearly into the next room. A **Perception check (DC 10)** will note scratch marks on the inside of both the south and north doors of this room. Upon reaching the center of the room, the PCs trigger a variation of the [Slamming Portal Haunt](https://sites.google.com/site/pathfinderogc/gamemastering/haunts/cr-1/slamming-portal-cr-1). Both doors slam shut (perhaps shutting other PCs out), and remain closed as if affected by a [Hold Portal](https://sites.google.com/site/pathfinderogc/magic/all-spells/h/hold-portal) spell. Any PC in the door's way must make a **Reflex save (DC 12)** to jump out of the way or be dealt 1d4 bludgeoning damage and moved into the room-appropriate square. > A ghostly and flaming human apparition appears in the middle of the room, holds his head, and begins to scream. The walls, ceiling, and floors burst into flame as the room becomes unbearably hot. The room appears to burst into flame, although a **Will Save (DC 11)** dismisses this as an illusion. Illusion or no, the room deals one point of fire damage to all in the room at the start of each character's turn. The doors will remain shut and the heat will persist for one minute, or 10 rounds. During this time, the PCs have a variety of options, one of which is breaking down either door. The PCs can make a **Strength check (DC 18)** to break down the doors, a DC which includes the +5 bonus from [Hold Portal](https://sites.google.com/site/pathfinderogc/magic/all-spells/h/hold-portal). The doors have 5 hardness and 10 hit points. If our heroes are able to break down one or both of the doors, the haunt immediately ends. It is also possible to deal with the Haunt directly. With only two hit points, channeling positive energy or throwing holy water will likely "kill" it, as well as a force spell such as magic missile. ### Room 5: Making a Living > You enter a plesant room. Lavish paintings cover the walls, piles of gold sit sparkling in the corners and a crystal chandelier hangs from the ceiling. At the north end of the room is a large stone statue with three closed eyes and three outstretched hands pointing to three squares on the ground. On each closed eyelid is a carved picture. On each square is a carved phrase. There is also a pile of seemingly random items along the east wall. Room 5 is ornate to say the least. Unfortunately, these are all permanent [Silent Images](http://www.d20pfsrd.com/magic/all-spells/s/silent-image/) and a simple **Will save (DC 10)** will reveal them as such. There is also a pile of seemingly random items, such as a shoe, a bow, a hat, a bear trap, a properly addressed letter with nothing but gibberish on it, and a lockpick. These items all look shoddy and quickly made, though they are not illusions. This pile is the previously crafted items of heroes to pass the “Craft for me” challenge. At the north end of the room is a large stone statue. Each eye of the statue represents a challenge. Passing all three challenges and opening all three eyes results in the statue opening its mouth. Inside the mouth is a lever. Once flipped, a magical cord running from the lever into the western wall will illuminate. Back in Room 1, the cord running from hidden door to the eastern wall will illuminate. On each closed eyelid is a carved picture. Written on each of these squares is a phrase in common: #### West Square: Entertain Me > Written on this square is the phrase, "Entertain me." The closed eyelid facing this direction shows a man with a fiddle and a woman telling jokes at a bar. In this square, one of our heroes must make a **Perform check (DC 10)**. Alternatively, they may make a DC 15 Diplomacy, Bluff, or Intimidate check to tell some sort of joke or story. *They may also simply impress the GM with their role-playing prowess.* Succeeding at this will cause the relevant statue’s eye to open. Failing will result in bolt of electricity dealing 1d4 damage (**Reflex (DC 12) for half**) to the person on the square. #### Center Square: Work for Me > Written on this square is the phrase, "Work for me." The closed eyelid facing this direction shows a woman counting coins at a bank and a man teaching a classroom full of students. In this square, one of our heroes must make a **Profession check (DC 10)** while somehow demonstrating his skill. If a hero has a backstory of making a living using some other skill, he may demonstrate that skill at a DC 15. Succeeding at this check will cause the relevant statue’s eye to open. Failing will result in bolt of electricity dealing 1d4 damage (**Reflex (DC 12) for half**) to the person on the square. #### East Square: Craft for Me > Written on this square is the phrase, "Craft for me." In this square, our heroes must make a **Craft check (DC 10)** while demonstrating their skill. {{#MarkdownIt}} ### Room 7: Choice of Gemstones > Behind the hidden door are two pedestals with one gemstone each. A sign in Common reads “You may only choose one.” One gemstone is worth 500gp, and the other is worth 100gp. An **Appraise check (DC 10)** will yield the 500gp gemstone, while failing the Appraise check yields the 100gp gemstone. The GM should make this roll in private, simply letting the PCs know which one they think is more valuable. Touching one gemstone turns the other to dust, so greedy heroes who try and grab both will wind up with neither. ### Room 8: The Many Paintings of Malebolge > The room breaks off into five short hallways, each with a painting at the end. In the center of the room is a small pedestal with a single word written in Common, “Malebolge.” A line beneith it is written in another language. If a character can identify the writing as Infernal, either by knowing Infernal or performing a **Linguistics check (DC 15)** to recognize the writing, then all Knowledge checks regarding the paintings are made at a +2 bonus. At the end of each of the hallways is a detailed and impressive painting. These paintings are magically attached to the walls, though they can be removed with a **Strength check (DC 25)** and sold for 100 gold a piece. Our heroes may examine the paintings and make the relevant knowledge check without going down the hallway. Failing the knowledge check for the wrong hallways will result in the players thinking that perhaps they have heard the word “Malebolge” in relation to the painting. Succeeding at the knowledge check for the wrong hallways will let the players know the painting has nothing to do with the word. Going down the wrong hallways springs an [Arrow Trap](http://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/traps-hazards-and-special-terrains/traps/arrow-trap-cr-1/). Finding one of these Arrow Traps will not result in automatically finding the rest, as they are each hidden in different places. Remember that Knowledge checks of DC 10 or lower can be made untrained. #### The Cleric > At the end of the hallway is a painting of a Dwaven cleric holding a war hammer. He has many rings in his beard, and is wearing charcoal leather armor. He sits atop a throne of skulls, drenched in blood. A **Knowledge (Religion) check (DC 10)** will let the players know that this is Cleric of Khorne, and probably has nothing to do with “Malebolge.” Any worshipers of Khorne in the party will immediately recognize this without any check. #### The Island > At the end of this hallway is a map of an island. There is a mountain in the middle and a single port city on the northern coast. On the mountain, the location of ancient ruins are clearly marked. A **Knowledge (Geography) check (DC 10)** will reveal that this is the Isle of Seed, and likely has nothing to do with “Malebolge.” #### The King > At the end of this hallway is a formal portrait of a great king. With a short brown goatee, golden crown and piercing green eyes, he is a slightly unnerving sight. A **Knowledge (Nobility) check (DC 10)** reveals that this is Lord Gyr of House Gixx, former Primarch of Sliccidon, and that he has nothing to do with Malebolge. #### Malebolge > At the end of this hallway is a painting of a disturbing scene. Injured and terrified people flee from giant red humanoids in a great forest where snow-like ash is falling. In the background is a giant flaming creature with spiked armor and gauntlets. A **Knowledge (Planes) check (DC 10)** will reveal that this place is Malebolge, the 6th layer of the Infernal Plane, and that the creature in the back is Moloch, ruler of its armies. If a PC goes to the end of this hallway, the painting will burn away revealing room 9. #### Tiamat > At the end of this hallway is a painting of a five-headed dragon being drawn and quartered, bleeding light in all directions. A **Knowledge (History) check (DC 10)** will reveal that this event was known as the Shattering of Tiamat, and probably has nothing to do with Malebolge. ### Room 9: Corridor of the Dead > This room has no lit torches. You must provide your own light if you want to see. A **Perception check (DC 20)** will reveal that the ground here has been worn down by countless footsteps (the two undead enemies slowly revolving around the center). As soon as our heroes enter the room, the Ghoul will begin to move clockwise and the Zombie will begin to move counter clockwise. They each move one square a turn. If they gain line of sight to the party, they will begin moving as normal and rush in to attack. The Ghoul has no hesitations about coup de gras-ing paralyzed players, and one or two players could easily die in this fight if the rolls go poorly. A small locked treasure chest is at the center of the room. The lock is simple enough, requiring a **Disable Device check (DC 20)**. Inside is 200 gold worth of strange silver coins, a harrow deck, and one use of arsenic. On the north wall, there is a lever. If it is flipped, a magical cord extending to the eastern wall begins to glow. {{#MarkdownIt}} ### Room 10: Bridge Over Troubled Rats > In the middle of this dark room is a rickety rope bridge that extends over a 20 foot deep pit to a narrow ledge on the north side. At the bottom of the pit, you can hear the scampering and squeaking of a Rat Swarm. With the levers in Rooms 9 and 5 switched, the hidden door in Room 1 opens. As long as there are no PCs in the pit, the Rat Swarm gains regeneration 10 as rats will enter from the holes in the walls to replace their fallen comrades. There is no light in Room 10 save what our heroes bring, but they can certainly hear the rats scampering and squeaking in the dark. Crossing this rickety bridge requires an **Acrobatics check (DC 10)** to move the PC’s speed. A PC may travel at half their speed or lower with an **Acrobatics check (DC 5)**. Fail these checks and the PC falls into the pit where they take 20 feet of fall damage (1d6) and must contest with the Rat Swarm. By using a length of rope and a **Knowledge (Engineering) or Use Rope check (DC 10)**, our heroes can reinforce the bridge and decrease the Acrobatics DC by 2 each. The PC may climb out of the pit by climbing up a rickety rope ladder with a successful **Climb check (DC 5)**. {{/MarkdownIt}}

{{#MarkdownIt}} ### Room 11: Posy the Enchantress Room 11 has six columns with hard corners. A female human named Posy hides in the shadows at 11b. She was once dressed in lavish furs and red ribbons, but these have long since been reduced to tatters. Long ago, Posy was an adventurer who failed to disbelieve the illusions of wealth in Room 5. She thought that she had come across great treasure, and killed her adventuring party to keep it for herself. She now waits in Jacob’s Tower for other adventurers, and loots their bodies to add to her imaginary riches. Little does she know that each time she is slain, the Tower brings her back to life as a challenge for the next adventuring party. Posy will target the first person who enters the room with a charm person spell as covertly as possible. As long as she has not yet been seen, she will not yet suffer the -5 penalty to DC for being in combat. If she succeeds, she will quickly attempt to bluff her new ally into thinking that the other PCs have been enchanted and need to be subdued but not harmed. This order constitutes an opposed Charisma check. As the fight progresses, she will cast invisibility on herself, move to a safe location, then begin to use bardic performance. She may also switch to a heavy crossbow as appropriate. Given that Posy does not actually have a Ring of Protection +1, her AC is actually 17, 13 touch, 14 flatfooted. If Posy successfully subdues her enemies, she will instruct her “friends” to go get help from outside. As the last one crosses through Room 10, she will shake the bridge, incurring an Acrobatics 15 check to stay on. Posy will then start to shoot the remaining PCs with her crossbow. If she takes down her “friends,” then she will go around coup-de-gracing the fallen PCs. Assuming the heroes live through this encounter, they can loot poor Posy. Ignore Posy's stated gear. Instead, she has a masterwork dagger, a masterwork heavy crossbow with 20 bolt, a masterwork chain shirt, absinthe, a potion of invisibility, two potions of jump, and a wand of cure light wounds with 5 charges (CL1). After defeating Posy, a stone staircase (11a) rises up from the ground. This staircase leads to the second Level, Marble and Glass. If our heroes are playing only in Jacob's Tower and ranking up to Level 2, then distribute 940 gold amongst them evenly. {{/MarkdownIt}}

The sound will get louder for two rounds until three Grimples burst into the room and attack. Every additional night they will be attacked by one more Grimple: Four the second night, five the third night, and so on. {{/MarkdownIt}}
